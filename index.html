Timeline

1960's

Two brothers, Stanley and Sidney Goldstein, along with partner, Ralph Hoagland, would embark on a journey of success. In Lowell, Massachusetts, the three businessmen would open the first Consumer Value Store. An establishment for individuals who seek beauty and health products. Later, they would begin to work with pharmacy departments, expanding their products to pharmaceutical medicines. Their extension from health products to pharmaceutical items, would differentiate CVS from other stores that sell similar items. Resulting them being on top of their competitions.

1970's

The company reached its stores in New England. By that time, they would operate 100 stores, and receive $100 million in annual sales. The sudden success of CVS would be inserted into shopping malls.

1980's

Stanley Goldstein, co-founder, would go on and be COO and President of Melville Corporation, a company that bought CVS back in 1969. That decade was also the 25th Anniversary of CVS and achieved $1.6 Billion in annual sales.

1990's

CVS acquired 500 stores from People’s Drug, which established the company in new mid-Atlantic markets. CVS also acquired 500 stores from People’s Drug, which established the company in new mid-Atlantic markets. Following the restructuring of Melville Corporation, CVS Corporation became a standalone company trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the CVS ticker.

2000's

CVS/pharmacy introduces the ExtraCare Card, becoming the first national pharmacy retailer to launch a loyalty card program. It also completes its acquisition of 1,268 Eckerd Stores and Eckerd Health Services, Eckerd’s mail order and pharmacy benefit management business. CVS/pharmacy partners with MinuteClinic, announcing three clinics to open in CVS/pharmacy stores. CVS Corporation and Caremark Rx, Inc. complete their transformative merger, creating CVS Caremark, the nation’s premier integrated pharmacy services provider.

2010's

In March, Larry Merlo succeeded Tom Ryan as President and CEO of CVS Caremark. Merlo joined CVS/pharmacy in 1990 through the acquisition of People’s Drug. CVS Caremark announces that it exceeded $100 billion in revenues in 2011. CVS Caremark announces that its corporate name has changed to CVS Health to further reflect its broader commitment to health care.
